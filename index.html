Personas Project CS1300

Inference Sketch

  This is a sketch of the interface for a rice cooker. It is comprised of a series of
  buttons and a small screen that tells the time when not displaying the output of a button
  push. Using the buttons and display on the screen the user can choose from several options
  of how to cook their rice depending on what they are putting into the machine and how long
  they want it to cook for.

User Questions and Observations

                  User 1

                  Observations

                  - Seemed confident when operating 
                  - Immediately directed their attention to the screen over the buttons
                  - Pressed multiple buttons to see the results on the screen as if they were exploring the options but not entering in a specific cooking sequence
                  
                  Questions

                  - Have you used this type of appliance before?
                    - Yes

                  - Does this interface remind you of interfaces on similar appliances?
                    - Yes, its a lot like other kitchen appliances such as a blender, crockpot, or coffee pot

                  - Were there any buttons that you noticed first? If so, why?
                    - The cooking settings because it has more features than mine

                  - Were there any buttons or labels that seemed confusing?
                    - I found it surprising that the menu button is small even though thats a key component

                  - Did the colors have any impact on your attention and immediate response?
                    - I felt comfortable with the red color for cook and the orange color for keep warm because it followed the standard that Im used to for these things
                  
                  
                  User 2

                  Observations

                  - Seemed hesitant when operating
                  - Asked permission before turning it on
                  - Would take their hand off after pushing a single button and look for a response as opposed to other uesers who kept their hand on the interface and 
                    pushed buttons in a faster sequence. 
                  
                  Questions

                  - Have you used this type of appliance before?
                    - No

                  - Does this interface remind you of interfaces on similar appliances?
                    - Kind of like an oven I guess

                  - Were there any buttons that you noticed first? If so, why?
                    - The cook and keep warm button because theyre large and towards the top

                  - Were there any buttons or labels that seemed confusing?
                    - Figuring out how the buttons correspong to whats on the screen

                  - Did the colors have any impact on your attention and immediate response?
                    - Yeah I noticed the red colored button for cook but not really besides that. The blue color looks nice though
                  
                  
                  User 3

                  Observations

                  - Seemed comfortable with interacting but not confident in using
                  - Joked that they "hope they dont break it"
                  - Would wait for a responce in a cautious way after clicking certain buttons such as raising the temperature                                         
                  
                  Questions

                  - Have you used this type of appliance before?
                    - No

                    - Yes, it reminds me of my air fryer back home

                  - Were there any buttons that you noticed first? If so, why?
                    - The cook button because its large and colored red

                  - Did the colors have any impact on your attention and immediate response?
